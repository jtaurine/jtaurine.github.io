Some elements of theory¶

In order to split dissipative and non-disspative effects, on can split the total magnetic field \(\pmb{h}\) into a reversible \(\pmb{h}_\mathrm{r}\) and irreversible \(\pmb{h}_\mathrm{i}\) part such that

(1)¶\[\pmb{h}=\pmb{h}_\mathrm{r}+\pmb{h}_\mathrm{i}.\]

Magnetization is related to \(\pmb{h}_\text{r}\) following the constituve law

(2)¶\[\pmb{m}=\text{M}_\text{an}(\pmb{h}_\mathrm{r})\]

where \(\text{M}_\text{an}\) is an arbitrary function that describe the non-dissipative behavior. The magnetization dissipation is

(3)¶\[\mathcal{D}=\mu_0 \pmb{m} \cdot \dot{\pmb{h}_\text{i}}\]

with \(\pmb{m}_\text{i}\) the variable associated with \(\pmb{h}_\text{i}\) and \(\mu_0\) the permeability of free space. To ensure that \(\mathcal{D}\) is always positive, one can consider the evolution law

(4)¶\[\dot{\pmb{h}}_\text{i}=\dot{\lambda} \boldsymbol{\chi}^{-1}\cdot\pmb{m}_\text{i}\]

with \(\dot{\lambda}\) being a positive multiplier and \(\boldsymbol{\chi}\) a positie definite magnetic susceptibility tensor. We consider

(5)¶\[\dot{\lambda}=\dfrac{1}{\tau_1}\left(1-\dfrac{\pmb{h}_\text{i}}{H_\text{c}} \cdot \pmb{e}_{\dot{\pmb{h}}}\right)\]

and

(6)¶\[\dot{\pmb{m}}_\text{i}=\tau_{2}\dot{\pmb{m}}.\]

The full hysteresis model is

(7)¶\[\begin{split}\begin{cases} \pmb{h}_\mathrm{r}=\pmb{h}-\pmb{h}_\mathrm{i}. \\ \dot{\pmb{h}}_\text{i}=\dfrac{\tau_2}{\tau_1}\left(1-\dfrac{\pmb{h}_\text{i}}{H_\text{c}} \cdot \pmb{e}_{\dot{\pmb{h}}}\right) \boldsymbol{\chi}^{-1}\cdot\dot{\pmb{m}}\\ \pmb{m}=\text{M}_\text{an}(\pmb{h}_\mathrm{r}) \end{cases}\end{split}\]
