Thermodynamics and constitutive laws¶

Legendre transformation¶

Considering thermo-magnetic-mechanical physics, the arguments for the internal energy density \(u\) at any scale are the following extensive thermodynamic variables: the entropy density \(s\), the magnetization \(\pmb{m}\) and the total strain \(\boldsymbol{\epsilon}\).

(1)¶\[\dot u (\pmb{m},\boldsymbol{\epsilon},s)=\mu_0\pmb{h}\cdot \dot{\pmb{m}}+\boldsymbol{\sigma}:\dot{\boldsymbol{\epsilon}}+T\dot s\]

Using Legendre transformation, one can define Helmoltz free energy density \(f\) such that

(2)¶\[f(\pmb{m},\boldsymbol{\epsilon},T)=u-Ts.\]

Another Legendre transformation allows to define free enthalpy density

(3)¶\[\Psi(\pmb{m},\boldsymbol{\sigma},T)=f-\boldsymbol{\sigma}:\boldsymbol{\epsilon}.\]

Using (1), (2) and (3), one can identify the equations of state

(4)¶\[\frac{\partial \Psi(\pmb{m},\boldsymbol{\sigma},T)}{\partial \pmb{m}}=\mu_0 \pmb{h}, \quad \frac{\partial \Psi(\pmb{m},\boldsymbol{\sigma},T)}{\partial \boldsymbol{\sigma}}=-\boldsymbol{\epsilon}, \quad \frac{\partial \Psi(\pmb{m},\boldsymbol{\sigma},T)}{\partial T}=-s.\]

It is sometimes more convenient to have a function of \(\pmb{h}\) instead of \(\pmb{m}\). The Gibbs free energy is then defined as

(5)¶\[g(\pmb{h},\boldsymbol{\sigma},T)=\Psi-\mu_0\pmb{h} \cdot \pmb{m}\]

where the second therm denotes the Zeeman energy. The equations of state can be used to write the constitutive laws even if it is not mandatory.
